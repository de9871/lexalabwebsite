About the Lab

Rigorous Science,
Human-Centered Purpose

At LEXA Lab, we combine rigorous cognitive science with careful attention to neurodevelopmental differences, studying how children learn, communicate, and develop literacy skills so that findings translate into usable tools for families and clinicians.

Campus / Lab Photo

"We study how children learn and communicate, with the goal of making research findings accessible to families, educators, and the clinicians who work with them."

Our Mission

The LEXA Lab focuses on understanding and promoting language, literacy and communication development in autistic children, with a particular focus on the interaction with executive function. The overarching goal of all the research projects is to empower autistic children to become effective learners and communicators.

Discovery

Uncovering the fundamental mechanisms of how children learn and process information in their natural, everyday environments.

Inclusion

Ensuring research reflects the diverse experiences of all families, particularly those from underrepresented and underserved communities.

Application

Translating lab findings into practical, usable tools for educators, clinicians, and parents who work directly with children every day.

What We Study

Our research is organized around questions that families, educators, and clinicians regularly ask about children's development.

question_mark

How do children learn to communicate?

We investigate the early precursors to language, including how infants use gestures, eye contact, and vocalizations to share their world with others before they say their first words. Understanding these early steps helps us identify children who may need additional support.

question_mark

What makes literacy development different for children with autism?

We study how language processing, phonological awareness, and executive function shape literacy outcomes in children with autism, including both challenges and strengths that are often overlooked in traditional assessments.

question_mark

How does executive function support everyday communication?

Skills like attention, working memory, and cognitive flexibility play a significant but often overlooked role in communication and language learning. We study how these skills interact with communication outcomes in children with autism and children with developmental language differences.

question_mark

How can we build on a child's strengths?

Our goal is to identify what children can do, not only where they face difficulty. We look for ways to build on existing strengths and give families and educators concrete information they can use.

Designed With Your Family in Mind

We know that participating in research requires trust, and we take that seriously. Our studies are designed to be sensory-friendly and respectful of your child's and family's individual needs.

  • verified Sensory-calm, welcoming lab environments
  • verified Scheduling designed to work for your family whenever possible
  • verified Clear communication about what to expect before, during, and after each visit
  • verified Participation is always voluntary and confidential

Student Opportunities

Training the Next Generation

LEXA Lab is an active training environment for graduate and undergraduate students at GSU. We provide hands-on experience in study design, data collection, behavioral coding, and advanced analytical methods.

Students who train with us develop skills in developmental science, communication research, and clinical reasoning, preparing them for graduate programs, research careers, and clinical practice.
