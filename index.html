Our Mission

Lexa Lab is a research group at Georgia State University studying language, literacy, and executive function in children with autism and children with neurodevelopmental differences. We work with families to understand how children communicate, learn to read, and build skills, and to bring those findings to families, educators, and clinicians.

Core Research Focus

Communication & Language

We investigate how children learn words and build sentences, looking for the most effective ways to support diverse learners in their earliest years before formal schooling begins.

Autism & Neurodiversity

Our work honors neurodiverse perspectives, exploring executive function and cognitive profiles unique to children with autism and adolescents, using a strengths-based approach.

Family-Centered Research

Our studies are designed to be accessible and respectful. We aim for findings that are relevant to families, schools, and caregivers, not just the academic literature.

Why Our Research Matters

Research isn't just about data. It's about what the findings can do. Our work aims to develop better educational resources, more effective support strategies, and a clearer understanding of how children with autism develop language and literacy.

  • check_circle Evidence-based strategies for teachers, caregivers, and clinicians
  • check_circle Advancing the understanding of neurodiversity and communication
  • check_circle Direct impact on children and families in Georgia and across the country
  • check_circle Training the next generation of researchers and clinicians at GSU
